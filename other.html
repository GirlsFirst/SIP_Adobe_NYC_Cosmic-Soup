Amelia A J Foy



Art is dissent. Art is rebellion. But art is also deeply personal.

A lot of mine is very expressive and has always been a tool to cope with my thoughts and emotions, especially as a neurotic bisexual young woman in a society telling me I, on the most part, should not exist (I still have my cis whiteness going for me). It's a medium you have control over, that you can mould so it fits "your" narrative, not the one imposed on you: that cishet neurotypical whiteness is the "norm" when in reality, it's not -- other voices are simply silenced, and this illusion isolates minorities further. There is power to be found in defying this, and loud unapologetic social activism is valuable and necessary; however, as a socially anxious person, it can be incredibly hard to do this.

So art for me bridges this gap by allowing me to scream and cry and connect with others like me without literally having to do so.

Amelia A J Foy, tumblr
